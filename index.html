Software Engineering Student

Biography

I was born and raised in Allentown, Pennsylvania by two immigrant parents from Mexico, Maria and Antonio Castaneda. Despite living in Allentown until I was 13-years-old, I never attended their school district. My parents, who were worried about Allentown's reputation for drugs and violence insisted that I commute 15 minutes to the small town Catasauqua, where I would eventually graduate from. Catasauqua, a school which graduates approximately 100 students a year would be where I developed my longest standing passion, fitness. Having joined the Football and Wrestling teams for the first time in my life, I learned the importance of physical fitness and facing adversity.

After graduating from High School (barely) and uncertain of the next step, I reluctantly enrolled at Northampton Community College. Overnight I went from a careless student to enthusiastic learner, earning myself a 3.53 GPA in my first year at College. However, this second wind as a learner would not last as the following year I would be dropped from my summer courses for inability to pay tuition dues.

Suddenly going from proud student to warehouse worker crushed my spirit and led me to an identity crisis. Without education who was I? I lost all motivation, I stopped attending the gym, stopped improving myself, and became nihilistic. During this low point is when I realized how remarkable my support system was. With the support of my parents Maria and Antonio and my sisters Selena and Alejandra, I regained the drive to better myself.

I had to go back to what initially gave me confidence, fitness. I began practicing calisthenics and started attending a Muay Thai gym. Muay Thai, known as the Art of Eight Limbs, transformed my body and my dietary practices, but most importantly bolstered my self-esteem and mental health.

I had heard about Python and coding from my sisters' friends with careers in software engineering. I was captivated by their work/life balance, their salaries, and their happiness. I was sold, but despite being given leads on how to start learning I was reluctant. I had a hard time committing to an uncertain learning method. My sister Selena, who works in HR, discovered FlatIron after hiring software engineering roles at her company. I decided that I was done waiting, and that I would dive in and become the software engineer I was inspired to become.
